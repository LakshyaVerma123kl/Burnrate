# Go-to-Market Strategy

**Target User**
VP of Engineering or fractional CTOs at Series A/B startups (20–150 employees). These are the people responsible for software infrastructure budgets, and at this scale, AI tool spend is large enough to matter but chaotic enough to be unmanaged.

**Trigger Event**
They are looking at their monthly Ramp or Brex statement, see a huge blended charge for "OpenAI + Anthropic + GitHub", and Google: *"How to track AI tool spend"* or *"Are we overpaying for Cursor?"*

**Where they hang out**
- **Specific Subreddits:** r/SaaS, r/startups, r/devops
- **Slack/Discord Communities:** YC founder Slack, CTO Craft, local tech hubs
- **Newsletters:** TLDR Web Dev, ByteByteGo

**Getting the first 100 users in 30 days ($0 budget)**
1. **The "Tear-down" Strategy**: I will run Burnrate audits on publicly available data of 5 well-known startups or open-source projects, detailing exactly how they are burning money on AI.
2. **Distribution**: Post these teardowns as detailed threads on X and Hacker News, ending with "Run your own stack through Burnrate to see your leak."
3. **Direct Outreach**: Cold DM 50 Series A founders on LinkedIn offering a free custom audit report (done via the tool).

**The Unfair Distribution Channel**
Credex's existing network. Credex can inject Burnrate as a "Free Health Check" into the onboarding flow of their current customers' sub-companies or partner networks. We can also build a Chrome extension that sits on the AWS/Ramp billing page and injects a "See if you can save 20% on this bill" button.

**Week-1 Traction Metrics**
- 500 unique visitors from the launch threads.
- 150 completed audits (30% conversion from view to run).
- 30 emails captured (20% conversion from run to lead).
- 5 high-intent Credex consultations booked.
