# Unit Economics

If Credex deployed Burnrate tomorrow, here is the math to profitability.

**Value of a Converted Lead**
Assuming a high-savings lead ($500+/mo savings) converts into a Credex customer buying $5,000 worth of infrastructure credits annually at a 15% margin, the Gross Profit per converted lead is **$750/year**.

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) by Channel**
1. **Viral Shareable URLs (Organic):** $0
2. **Cold Outreach (LinkedIn/Email):** ~$50 per booked meeting (cost of tools like Apollo/Instantly + SDR time).
3. **Twitter/HN Teardowns:** ~$0 (founder time).

**Conversion Funnel**
To be profitable, we need the following metrics:
- 1,000 visitors land on Burnrate
- 20% complete the audit (200 audits)
- 10% of audits are "high-savings" (20 high-savings cases)
- 25% of high-savings cases submit their email (5 leads)
- 20% of leads convert to paying Credex customers (1 customer)
  
*Result:* 1,000 visitors = 1 customer = $750 Gross Profit.
This means we can afford to spend up to **$0.75 per visitor** on paid channels if organic traction slows down.

**Path to $1M ARR in 18 Months**
To reach $1M in Gross Profit via this channel alone:
- We need 1,333 converted customers ($1,000,000 / $750).
- Using our funnel math (0.1% visitor-to-customer conversion), we need 1.33 million visitors over 18 months, or ~74,000 visitors/month.
- *What must be true:* The viral loop of the shareable URL (benchmark sharing, "Look how much we saved") must achieve a viral coefficient > 0.3 to artificially inflate the top-of-funnel without crushing our CAC.
